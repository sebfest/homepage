Hello! I’m Sebastian Fest, a data engineer at the Directorate of Fisheries in Bergen. I work on building and maintaining data pipelines, databases, and analytics solutions that support the management of marine resources. My daily work involves handling large-scale fisheries statistics, regulatory data, and other information critical to sustainable resource management. I mainly use Python and SQL, working with a mix of on-prem infrastructure and cloud solutions such as Oracle and Azure.

My path into data engineering grew out of a background in research and data management. Before joining the Directorate, I worked at the Centre for Experimental Research on Fairness, Inequality and Rationality (FAIR), where I designed workflows for data collection, supported research projects, and contributed to research infrastructure. I also developed open-source tools such as ExpMotor for participant recruitment and pyturk for integrating workflows with AWS MTurk.

I hold a PhD in Behavioral Economics from the Norwegian School of Economics, an M.Sc. from the University of Bergen, and an intermediate diploma from the University of Potsdam. My academic research has explored topics such as inequality, moral motivation, gender differences, and incentive structures. For those interested, a selection of past projects and papers is available on the research page.

I live with my family in Bergen, where we embrace the relentless rain of western Norway. You can find the latest version of my CV here.
